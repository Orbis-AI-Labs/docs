Orbis includes a built-in investor database — firms and individual investors you can browse, filter, bookmark, and enroll in outreach campaigns.

## Browsing investors

Navigate to **Investors** in the sidebar to open the investor table. The database includes two kinds of records:

- **Firms** — VC funds, angel groups, and institutional investors
- **People** — individual investors, partners, and angels

Use the **Kind** toggle at the top to switch between All, Firms, and People.

## Investor profiles

Click any investor to open their profile sidebar with:

  <Card title="Investment focus" icon="target">

Firm profiles also show fund size, fund year, and a Crunchbase link. People profiles show their company and job title.

## Filtering and search

The filter builder lets you narrow down investors by:

- **Name** — text search
- **Kind** — firm or person
- **Type** — VC, Angel, PE, Family Office, etc.
- **Stage** — Pre-Seed, Seed, Series A, B, C, Growth, etc.
- **Sector focus** — SaaS, Fintech, Healthcare, Climate, etc.
- **Location** — city, country, or region
- **Check size** — investment range
- **Geography focus** — where they invest
- **Email** — has or doesn't have an email

### Saved lists

Save any filter combination as a named list using the **Save List** button. Saved lists appear in the sidebar for quick access.

## Bookmarking investors

Select one or more investors using the checkboxes, then click **Save Selected** to bookmark them. Bookmarked investors are saved to your personal list and can be filtered separately.

## Adding investors to campaigns

To enroll an investor in an outreach campaign:

1. The investor must have an **email address** on file
2. Click the investor's row action menu and select **Add to Campaign**
3. Orbis creates a contact record from the investor's data and adds them to the selected campaign

<Note>
  Converting an investor to a contact is a one-way operation — it creates a new contact in your CRM with the investor's details so they can receive campaign emails.
</Note>

## Data source

The investor database is curated and maintained by Orbis. You cannot create, edit, or delete investor records directly — the data is read-only. Use bookmarks, notes, and tags to organize investors for your own workflows.
