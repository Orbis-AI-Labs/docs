A company record in Orbis represents an organization — a business, startup, nonprofit, or any other entity. Companies are linked to contacts, so you can navigate from a person to their employer and back, and track notes and interactions at the company level.

## What a company record contains

Each company record holds:

- **Name and domain** — the company's legal name and website domain
- **Logo** — automatically fetched via Brandfetch when a domain is known
- **Description** — a short summary of what the company does
- **Industry and size** — industry classification and employee count range
- **Location** — city, region, country, and metro area
- **Social profiles** — LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Facebook, and other public pages
- **Financial data** — funding stage, total funding raised, revenue range, stock ticker, market cap, and IPO year when available
- **Tech stack** — technologies the company uses, sourced from enrichment
- **Contacts** — all people in your network who work at this company

## The companies list

The companies list shows all organizations in your Orbis workspace. You can:

- **Search** by company name or domain
- **Filter** by industry, employee size, country, or whether a LinkedIn or website is present
- **Sort** by name, contact count, or date added
- **Switch views** between a table and a kanban board organized by lifecycle stage or relationship type

Each row shows the company logo, name, domain, industry, size, and the number of contacts linked to it.

### Relationship metadata

You can track your organization's relationship with each company using:

- **Relationship type** — customer, partner, vendor, investor, portfolio, prospect, competitor, or other
- **Lifecycle stage** — prospect, lead, opportunity, negotiation, committed, customer, churned, or dormant
- **Priority** — high, medium, low, or none
- **Sentiment** — positive, neutral, negative, or mixed
- **Owner** — the team member responsible for this relationship
- **Tags** — custom labels for grouping and filtering

## Adding companies

Companies can be added manually, via CSV import, or auto-created from contacts. See [Import Companies](/companies/import) for all import methods.

Orbis enriches companies automatically when a domain is known — pulling logos, financial data, social profiles, tech stack, and more. See [Company Enrichment](/companies/enrichment) for details.

## Pipeline view

Switch to the **Pipeline** view to see companies as a kanban board. Companies are arranged as cards across columns based on:

- **Lifecycle stage** (default) — prospect, lead, opportunity, negotiation, committed, customer, churned, dormant
- **Relationship type** — customer, partner, vendor, investor, etc.
- **Priority** — high, medium, low, none
- **Sentiment** — positive, neutral, negative, mixed

Drag cards between columns to update the field value. Each card shows the company logo, name, domain, contact count, and tags.

Use the column picker to switch which field controls the grouping. Filter and search within the pipeline view using the toolbar.

## Company detail cards

The company profile page includes several detail cards:

### Funding and financials
Funding stage, type, total funding, revenue range, stock ticker, and exchange information.

### Location
Full address, metro area, continent, and coordinates.

### Tech stack
Technology badges showing the tools and platforms the company uses.

### Website intelligence
An AI-generated summary of the company's website content, with a "last scraped" timestamp showing when the data was refreshed.

### Custom properties
Add arbitrary key-value data to any company — 9 field types including text, number, URL, date, currency, and percentage. Manage properties from the company profile.

### Brand data
Company brand colors and fonts when available from Brandfetch enrichment.

### Alternative names and domains
Alternative company names and domain aliases sourced from PeopleDataLabs.
